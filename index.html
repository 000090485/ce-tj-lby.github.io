Definition is a tool we use for everything. It is our safeguard. It allows us to grasp the feasibility of an existence without having to dig deeper. Once we define, we almost always accept. This outdated tool has existed for as long as language itself and it has cast populations into a sub-hypnotic mind-frame, taking away out natural tendency to question normality. The largest definition we hold universally religious in our society is society itself. We see it as a stationary matrix which cannot be altered by our actions - but this understanding is false, given to us as a result of defining ‘what is’ as ‘what is’.

The progression of society is a slow but exponential phenomenon, which we - as members of it - fail to recognise. It was only less than 10 years ago when the first iPhone first came out. But we still find it hard to recall a time where we couldn't check our email from our local Starbucks or post selfies on Instagram.

But progression isn't always a continuous uprising graph. There are stutters and temporary negative gradients, to which we as society are the regulators of.
